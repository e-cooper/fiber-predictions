Google Fiber Predictions

What did Kansas City look like before Google Fiber and what does it look like now? See how the other potential cities might benefit.

All Cities

Avg. Download Speeds of Potential Google Fiber Cities vs Kansas City (Jun '12 - Feb '14)

These charts show us the average download (Mbps) of internet in Kansas City as well as Atlanta, Nashville, Charlotte, Raleigh-Durham*, San Antonio, Salt Lake City, Portland, and San Jose for the past twenty (20) months. Google Fiber began the process of connecting homes to their network in Kansas City on Nov. 13, 2012 and shortly after, the city experienced a steady increase in average internet speeds. With Google's latest announcement of other cities as potential expansion areas, these graphs give us an idea of how these municipalities might benefit as a whole.
* Raleigh-Durham is the average speed of both Raleigh, NC and Durham, NC for the given month

Percent Increase in Avg. Download Speeds (Jun '12 - Feb '14) †

This chart shows us the percent of increase in internet speed for each of the previous cities over the same time period. Percent Increase in Avg. Download Speeds following Google Fiber Availability (from Nov '12)

This chart further breaks down the improvement Kansas City has seen in internet download speeds into three, six, and twelve month intervals. Using this information, we can finally make a prediction about how internet might improve over the same period of time for the other cities on the list.
